### [11974] Redesign Register Contract view

**State:** Done | **Assignee:** Charlie Bradley | **Priority:** 3 | **Tags:** Carried; design; No QA; UI/UX

**Description:**

It being one of our oldest UIs, we hear a lot of negative views of the Register Contract (edit profile plan) screen.  

 

 
The register contract screen is sooo ugly.  
It would be nice to rework it / simplify it / make it easier to use.  

 
Value prop:  
it's an eyesore 
we have the list on the left but the fields on the right are too numerous  
they get confused and don't do it and finding the scheduling people are entering this  
it could be simpler - let's dumb it on down a bit.  

 
What does success look like? More buyers registered by an SC versus a designer?  
 
what can we measure to indicate success?  
can we prompt them for feedback and log it?  

 
Workflow Diagrams in FigJam: https://www.figma.com/board/ukRl2uOn3m4XH3vvawTmKC/Edit-Profile-Plan?node-id=0-1&t=USt94Cu06zjuOYfI-1

**Acceptance Criteria:**

Produce a design for the edit profile plan / register contract view that fulfills the following use cases:  
register buyer contract  
add/update buyer contact info 
 
add financial info  
what others? some discussions with designers/sales folks may be useful here  
 The design should keep in mind a few design goals:  
for core use cases, the activity should be quick and simple  
fields are grouped somehow perhaps, with an overriding navigational concept  
validation should be intuitive (the current users dislike the validation on the left panel)  
 We would expect that discovery as a result of stakeholder interviews would revise these AC.
