### [29468] BioRev POC: Performance

**State:** Done | **Priority:** 1 | **Tags:** visualizer

**Description:**

During the PO demo to stakeholders, the performance of the BioRev POC was quite poor. Initially I assumed it was on their side, but then subsequent calls directly to their service in postman and a discussion with Justin revealed that we have some inefficiencies in how we are using it that cause the performance to be poor.  

 
In order to determine whether to proceed with them as a vendor, we need a more realistic picture of how the system will perform.  

 
Using the roger environment: 
First call - selecting a cabinet in the kitchen scene: ~17s  
Second call ~12s 
Third call ~12s 

 
Changing to bathroom scene, with selections already made: ~20s load time 
Removing a selection: ~15s  
Making a ctop selection: ~15s  

 

 
My conclusion is that we have somewhere between 6-11s of load time on our side over the API call.  

 
I think it was slower in my teams call, which also affected the performance.

**Acceptance Criteria:**

Investigate (document/profile) the sequence of calls from selection -> updated image for the provider and identify areas where we might want to refactor to improve timings (discuss with team as needed)  
If we identify small refactor steps that we can take, to more accurately reflect a production solution with respect to performance so that we can determine whether to proceed with the vendor on a pilot, and whether we need to ask them for various performance improvement changes (i.e. reduce image size fetch for rendered scene or just general rendering improvements)   
If the ideas we have about the refactor are not small enough to include in this work item, then they are at least described and estimated for future work.  
Given that the direct call for a room render is about 6 seconds, and the time to render after a selection in DMH is 12-15s, attempt to reduce that overhead and close the gap.
